Known as the 'King of Pop' and regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century, Micheal Jackson (August 29, 1958- June 25, 2009) was a best-selling American singer, songwriter and dancer who enjoyed a chart-topping career both with the Jackson 5 and as a solo artist. As a child, Jackson became the lead singer of his family's popular Motown group, the Jackson 5. He went on to a solo career of astonishing worldwide succes, delivering No. 1 hits from the albums Off the Wall, Thriller and Bad. He died in 2009 at age 50 of a drug overdose just before launching a comeback tour...
